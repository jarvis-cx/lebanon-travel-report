Analytical Assessment

All three governments are at maximum or near-maximum alert. Advisories were already elevated before the March 2 escalation. They will almost certainly remain elevated through at least Q2 2026. For August travel, you need to see: (1) a ceasefire holding for 2–3 months, (2) advisories downgraded to at minimum "Reconsider" (Australia) or "All but essential" (UK) for northern regions, and (3) Beirut airport operating normally. Travel insurance will be invalidated if you travel against "Do Not Travel" advice — this is the single biggest practical barrier.

Entry Requirements

For Australian passport holders. Verify all details before travel.

🛂 Visa on Arrival

  • ✓ Visa on arrival available for Australian citizens at Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport
  • ✓ Free 1-month visa stamped on arrival — extendable to 3 months at General Security offices
  • ✓ No eVisa system required — simply a stamp at immigration
  • ! Passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond date of entry
  • ! Children: same requirements — each child needs their own passport

✈️ Beirut Airport

  • • Rafic Hariri International Airport — the only commercial airport
  • • Immigration can be slow (30–60 min queues) but straightforward for Australians
  • • Airport is in southern Beirut suburbs — this area has been subject to airstrikes and evacuation orders in March 2026
  • • Current status (March 2026): "Open and operational" per FCDO, but extremely fluid. Airlines may cancel flights at short notice.
⚠️

Israeli Passport Stamp Warning

Lebanon refuses entry to anyone with Israeli stamps, visas, or evidence of travel to Israel in their passport.

  • • Israel no longer stamps passports (since 2013 — they use a paper entry slip)
  • • But: any Israeli visa sticker, or entry/exit stamps from land borders near Israel (Jordan/Egypt crossings), could cause issues
  • • If you've visited Israel, ensure your passport has no evidence of this
  • • If in doubt: renew your passport before travel

📋 What to Declare

  • • Currency: Amounts over USD $10,000 must be declared at customs
  • • Medications: Carry prescription medications in original packaging with a doctor's letter
  • • No specific additional declaration requirements for Australians beyond standard customs rules
  • • Photography restrictions: Avoid photographing military installations, government buildings, ports

☀️ August Heat (for ages 7 and 9)

Coast (Jounieh, Byblos)

27–32°C, high humidity (70–80%), virtually no rain. Hot but tempered by sea breeze.

Mountains (Bcharre, 1,400m)

22–28°C, much drier, pleasant. Can be cool in evenings. Excellent escape from coastal heat.

  • • UV Index: Very high (8–10). Sunburn risk is serious — SPF 50+ essential
  • • Avoid midday sun (12–3pm) with children
  • • Constant hydration required — bottled water only
  • • Consider scheduling Bcharre/mountain days mid-trip for relief from coastal heat

Family Considerations

Specific guidance for 2 adults + children ages 7 and 9.

🎡 Child-Friendly Attractions

Jounieh / Kesrouan

  • 🦇 Jeita Grotto

    Spectacular limestone caves with stalactites/stalagmites. Upper grotto on foot, lower grotto by boat. One of Lebanon's top attractions. ~20 min from Jounieh. Kids absolutely love it.

  • 🚡 Téléférique (Cable Car) to Harissa

    Cable car from Jounieh bay up to the Our Lady of Lebanon statue. Stunning views. Kids enjoy the ride (~10 min each way).

  • 🏖️ Jounieh Bay Beaches

    Sandy beaches, warm water in August. Beach clubs with full family facilities.

Byblos (Jbeil)

  • 🏰 Byblos Crusader Castle & Archaeological Site

    One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world (~7,000 years). Kids can explore ruins and castle walls. UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • 🛒 Byblos Old Souk

    Charming market streets, ice cream, crafts. Easy family strolling.

  • ⛵ Byblos Fishing Harbour

    Picturesque harbour, great for walks and photos. Good seafood restaurants nearby.

Bcharre / Qadisha Valley

  • 🌲 Cedars of God (Arz el-Rab)

    Ancient cedar forest, UNESCO World Heritage. Cooler mountain air (25°C). Easy walking trails suitable for ages 7+.

  • 🏞️ Qadisha Valley

    Dramatic gorge with monasteries. Choose easier trails — some sections are challenging for young children.

  • 💧 Baatara Gorge Waterfall

    Spectacular 3-tier waterfall ~1 hr from Bcharre. Viewing platform suitable for kids.

📋 What to Brief Kids On

  • • Only drink from sealed water bottles — never tap water or uncertain sources
  • • Stick with parents at all times in crowded markets and archaeological sites
  • • If something loud happens: find cover immediately, stay with adults, don't run
  • • Strangers being warm and touching your hair/giving sweets is normal Lebanese affection — not a threat
  • • No photography of military personnel, checkpoints, or official buildings
  • • Know the hotel name and address by heart

🥗 Food & Water Safety for Kids

  • • Only bottled water — including for brushing teeth
  • • Avoid raw salads washed in tap water
  • • Stick to freshly cooked food — avoid anything that looks like it has been sitting out
  • • Lebanese cuisine is generally child-friendly: hummus, flatbreads, grilled meats, falafel
  • • Ice cream from busy shops is generally fine
  • • Carry oral rehydration sachets (Gastrolyte) for gastro emergencies
  • • Lebanese culture: children are welcomed everywhere, including restaurants late at night

☀️ Heat Management (August)

Coast temperatures hit 30–32°C with high humidity. For ages 7 and 9:

  • • Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen every 2 hours; hats are essential
  • • Avoid outdoor activities between 12pm and 3pm — rest/pool time
  • • Carry 1.5L water bottles per child; refill constantly from sealed sources
  • • Plan mountain days (Bcharre) for relief: 22–25°C vs coastal 32°C
  • • Look for accommodation with pools — beach clubs and many hotels have them

🤝 Cultural Norms Around Children

  • • Lebanese culture is extremely child-friendly — children are adored and welcomed everywhere
  • • Strangers (shopkeepers, waiters, locals) may interact warmly: touching hair, offering sweets, pinching cheeks — cultural affection, not a threat
  • • Christian areas are relaxed about dress codes; modest clothing is appreciated but not required
  • • Children are welcomed in restaurants even for late dinners (Lebanese dining culture runs late)
  • • No specific safety concerns for Western children in northern Christian areas

🚗 Car Hire

  • • Available from Beirut airport and Jounieh: Sixt, Europcar, local companies
  • • Prices: ~$30–60/day for a mid-size SUV (recommended for mountain roads)
  • • International Driving Permit required alongside Australian licence
  • • Lebanese roads along the coast (Route 51M) are good quality
  • • Mountain roads to Bcharre: winding, narrow in parts, but paved. Scenic but demanding
  • • Child car seats: Bring your own or confirm with rental company — availability is inconsistent

🚕 Taxis & Ride-Hailing

  • • Bolt is the main ride-hailing app in Lebanon (Uber was available but inconsistent)
  • • Traditional taxis available — negotiate price before departure
  • • Shared "service" taxis run common routes cheaply
  • • For airport transfer: pre-book through your hotel — safer and easier with kids and luggage

⚠️ Driving Safety Warning

  • • Lebanon has one of the highest road fatality rates in the region
  • • Police presence is minimal; traffic laws are loosely enforced
  • • Driving culture: aggressive — lane markings are suggestions, horn use is constant, overtaking on blind corners is common
  • • Night driving on mountain roads is not recommended

💡 Recommended: Private Driver

With children, a private driver with car is well worth considering — removes road stress entirely and local drivers know the mountain roads. Many hotels can arrange this. Cost: ~$80–150/day for a driver with vehicle. Over a 2-week trip, factor this into your budget if you prefer not to drive yourself.

Money & Economy

💱 Currency Situation (March 2026)

The Lebanese Pound (LBP)

  • • Pre-crisis rate: 1,500 LBP per USD (before 2019)
  • • Current official rate: ~89,500 LBP per USD
  • • The Lebanese Pound lost ~98% of its value
  • • Now largely aligned (official vs market) after 2023 fix

Practical Reality

  • • USD is the de facto currency
  • • Most businesses price in USD, prefer USD cash
  • • Effectively a dollarized economy for visitors
  • • Do not bring Lebanese pounds from abroad — essentially worthless
💵

Bring USD Cash — Most Important Financial Advice

  • • Crisp, new bills preferred ($50 and $100 denominations work best)
  • • For 2 weeks, family of 4: bring $3,000–5,000 USD
  • • ATMs are unreliable for foreign cards — do not rely on them
  • • Credit cards accepted at major hotels and upscale restaurants, but many businesses are cash-only
  • • Money changers available everywhere with standardised rates

August-Specific Factors

☀️ August Weather

Coast — Jounieh, Byblos

27–32°C, humidity 70–80%, virtually no rain. Hot but tempered by sea breeze. Essentially rain-free — August is the hottest month.

Mountains — Bcharre (1,400m)

22–28°C, much drier, pleasant. Can be cool in evenings. A significant relief from coastal heat.

UV Index: Very high (8–10). Sunburn risk is serious — SPF 50+ and hats essential. Avoid outdoor activities 12–3pm.

🏖️ Peak Tourist Season

  • • August is peak Lebanese tourist season — Lebanese diaspora returns for summer holidays
  • • Hotels in Jounieh/Byblos at premium pricing and higher occupancy — book early
  • • Beach clubs will be crowded on weekends
  • • Mountain areas (Bcharre/Cedars) popular escape from coastal heat — expect visitors
  • • Prices for accommodation and activities are at their annual high
  • • Restaurants may be busier — consider earlier or later dining times

🎭 August Festivals & Events

July–August

Byblos International Festival 🏰

Major music festival held annually in the Crusader castle ruins. Returned in 2023 after a 3-year hiatus; 2025 edition was held. International and Lebanese artists. Amazing setting — one of the most atmospheric venues in the world. Family-friendly for earlier shows. Check byblosfestival.com for 2026 lineup/dates.

Usually July

Jounieh International Festival 🎆

Fireworks, concerts on the bay. Check local schedules for 2026 dates — may extend into August.

15 August

Assumption of Mary 🕊️

Major holiday in Christian Lebanon. Celebrations, processions, and church services throughout Jounieh, Byblos, and Bcharre. Festive, family-friendly atmosphere. One of the most important dates in the Lebanese Christian calendar.

🌙 Ramadan in August 2026?

No — Ramadan 2026 is in February/March.

Ramadan 2026 falls in February–March 2026. August is clear of Ramadan. No fasting restrictions or limited service hours to worry about. Christian-majority northern Lebanon is generally unaffected by Ramadan regardless.

🏊 Beach Season

August is the heart of Lebanon's beach season. Sea temperature: ~28°C — warm and very swimmable. Beach clubs along Jounieh Bay and near Byblos (Bahsa Beach) offer full facilities: sun loungers, restaurants, kids' pools, water activities. Weekends are very crowded; weekday mornings are the best time to visit.
